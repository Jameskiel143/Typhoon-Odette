Overview

Typhoon Odette, also known as Typhoon Rai, made landfall in the Philippines on December 16, 2021, causing widespread disaster across multiple regions. The quick escalation from a Category 1 to a Category 5 typhoon left little time for communities to prepare, resulting in major loss of life and extensive property damage. Below are the key aspects of Odette's impact.

Key Aspects:
Deaths: Over 400
Injuries: Around 1,000
Missing Persons: 70
Displaced People: 3 million
Destroyed Houses: Over 2 million
Estimated Damage: 24.6 billion PHP

Affected Areas:
The typhoon greatly affected Mindanao, specifically Siargao, and Typhoon Rai developed and made nine landfalls, leaving severe damage in five NDRRMC-identified districts. The provinces worst hit are Palawan, Negros Occidental, Bohol, Cebu, Negros Oriental, Southern Leyte, Leyte, Dinagat Islands, and Surigao del Norte.
