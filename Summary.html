Summary

Typhoon Odette, which hit the Philippines on December 16, 2021, caused massive damage and loss of life. The predicted damage to infrastructure and agriculture was 23.4 billion pesos, with the provinces of Palawan, Negros Occidental, Bohol, Cebu, Negros Oriental, Southern Leyte, Leyte, Dinagat Islands, and Surigao del Norte being among the most affected. The crisis left nearly 2.4 million people in need of help, highlighting the value of better disaster planning and strong infrastructure. Humanitarian groups like the World Food Programme and All Hands and Hearts were helpful in delivering emergency help, emphasizing the value of coordinated responses in disaster recovery.
